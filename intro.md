# Muen's data systems study notes

## About this notes repo and me
Hello, and welcome!
I’m Muen — a data engineer and a master’s student at Georgia Tech, wandering at the intersection of systems, data infrastructure, and climate tech.

My journey into data system and infra didn’t start from a traditional CS background, but from Architecture and Environmental Studies. I realized over time that infrastructure — whether physical or digital — has the same fundamental spirit:

:::
It’s unseen when it works well, painfully visible when it doesn’t, and utterly fascinating to build.
:::

After joining an energy-tech company and working on real data pipelines, distributed systems quirks, and the backstage machinery that powers analytics, I became obsessed with one question:

How do we design systems that stay reliable, scalable, and elegant even when reality is messy?

This study notes repo is my attempt to answer that question — slowly, imperfectly, but with curiosity.
Here you’ll find my learnings across:

- Data systemsm and infrastructure (Spark, Iceberg, Lakehouse design)
- Distributed systems
- Simulation & system dynamics
- And occasional detours into complex science, environmental system, and whatever else looks fun

Think of this as my personal textbook — evolving, incomplete, but always growing.
